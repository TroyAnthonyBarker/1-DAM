Hunger Levels

Learn to mark your degree of hunger!

It is a tool that can help control appetite, managing periods of fasting between meals and avoiding snacks due to anxiety, when it comes to eating healthy, avoiding weight gain and losing weight in case you have extra kilos

  1. Extreme hunger, starving, feeling weak and dizzy. It is urgent to feed you.
  2. Very hungry, with irritation, anxiety, low energy levels, stomach growling a lot. You would eat anything.
  3. Quite hungry, there is discomfort but you endure with some rumbling in your stomach.
  4. A little hungry, but under control, you can wait without problems.
  5. Neutral, feeling of satisfaction, neither feel hunger nor satiety.
  6. Satiety, the stomach is full but in a pleasant way, you could eat a little more.
  7. Full and satisfied, you don't want to eat more because you know it would make you uncomfortable.
  8. Very full, you feel uncomfortable and you can't eat anymore.
  9. Very full and upset, the stomach is swollen and you begin to feel pain and indigestion.
  10. To burst and bad, you feel sick with nausea and want to vomit.

Food Nutritional Scale

In nutrition, the food pyramid helps us to give a reference of the proportion that our body needs to maintain a healthy, balanced and balanced diet. It is recommended the daily intake of:

  • 55% carbs (variable depending on physical activity)
  • 30% fat
  • 15% protein, vitamins, minerals and fibers.
